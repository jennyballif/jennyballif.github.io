We make the kind of curriculum we wanted for our own kids: clear explanations, thoughtful projects, and lessons that build real understanding without busywork. We do almost everything ourselves so our courses stay personal, practical, and carefully designed.

How it all started

In 2013, Jenny volunteered to bring a weekly science demonstration to her son’s 2nd grade classroom. Her name changed from “Andrew’s Mom” to “Science Mom.” Within a year, she was visiting every classroom in the elementary school. She came to the school each week as a volunteer and did science demonstrations from opening bell to closing bell.

Soon Science Mom was visiting dozens of schools throughout the Las Vegas valley. When schools shut down in 2020, Science Mom convinced her partner Serge (a mathematician) to join her as “Math Dad” to host daily math and science lessons. That Quaran-time series was the Science Mom and Math Dad’s first time teaching classes together. They’ve been doing it ever since!

Who we are

Science Mom has worked as a molecular biologist and a wildland firefighter, and at several jobs that land in between wearing a lab coat and wielding a chainsaw. She has a bachelor's degree in crop science and a master's degree in plant science, both from Utah State University. Science Mom enjoys drawing and creates the doodle-style notes that accompany each course. She also loves being outside and especially enjoys hiking, identifying plants, and berry picking.

SMath dad grew up on a dairy farm in Southern Idaho. He has a PhD in mathematics from Penn State University and loves teaching all types of math, from arithmetic to advanced topics. He plays the violin and loves to sing. He also enjoys games and competitions. In each live class, he makes each quiz show a fun competition between him and the students (the Unbeatable Science Kids).

Science Mom and Math Dad live in Southeast Alaska. They have three kids and two adorable dogs (Kaladin and Lopen).

What we offer

We offer:

  • Full-year science and math courses. We teach the courses live, but afterward they are available as a self-paced class. We have a bundle of middle-school science classes that cover all of the middle school science standards.
  • A supportive community, including our weekly Chess Club and Painting with a Scientist series through Patreon
  • Free educational videos, printouts, and science lessons.

Most of our students are in upper elementary through middle school, but curious learners of any age are welcome.
